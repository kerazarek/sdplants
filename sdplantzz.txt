- _other cool plantstuff_
    - [San Diego](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_of_San_Diego) has a [mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_climate) (verging on [semi-arid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-arid_climate)) climate, so many of the ornamental and invasive plants around here are from other parts of the world with this type of climate and [biome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_forests,_woodlands,_and_scrub) (western coasts [around 30Â° latitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horse_latitudes), especially when there is a desert to the east), i.e. the [Mediterranean region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin) (Spain, [southern France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garrigue), Italy, Greece, North Africa, Israel, Lebanon, etc), the Canary islands, [central Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chilean_Matorral), the [cape region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fynbos) of South Africa, southern Australia. 
    - and many Californian plants are now invasive in those places (e.g. I saw North American agaves and cacti growing all over Israel)
    - that said, with an (arguably irresponsible) abundance of imported water and a climate that basically never gets below freezing, lots of other tropical plants are also grown here
    - many plants here are [sclerophylls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sclerophyll), a characteristic common to plants in this kind of climate around the world
    - many plants around here are drought-deciduous, losing their leaves in the rainless summer rather than in a freezing winter
    - a lot of succulents can grow a new plant from a single leaf so hypothetically one could stock a garden for cheap... (though of course I would _never_ take cuttings from someone else's garden without their permission)
    - agaves [bloom only once](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semelparity_and_iteroparity), sending up a flower-bearing "mast" and then dying ;(
